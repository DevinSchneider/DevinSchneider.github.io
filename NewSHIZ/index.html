The violin is a member of the string family, which also includes the viola, cello, and double bass. The violin is the smallest and highest-pitched member of the family. The violin is played with a bow, which is drawn across the strings to produce sound. The violin is used in a wide variety of musical styles, including classical, jazz, folk, and pop music.
